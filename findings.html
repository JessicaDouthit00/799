Results

EOD suits impose significant movement restrictions and exertion even during fundamental movements. While weighted vests replicate the challenges posed by EOD suits for trunk-dominant tasks such as squats and lunges, they do not adequately address the lower extremity specific demands associated with stepping tasks. The observed disparity in hurdle step performance underscores critical differences in weight distribution, with EOD suits loading both the torso and legs, whereas weighted vests load only the torso. This distinction carries important implications for training specificity and ecological validity.

Take Home:

Although equivalent weighted vests can serve as a cost-effective alternative to training in an EOD suit, vests cannot fully substitute for the specific movement demands required during EOD suit operations.
