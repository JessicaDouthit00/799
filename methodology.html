Methodology

Overview

60 recreationally resistance-trained healthy participants (age 23.3 ± 5.1 years; weight 82.3 ± 17.1 kg; height 175.6 ± 8 cm) participated in the study at the Human Performance Laboratory of Berea College for one day of testing. Participants completed a dynamic warm-up before conducting a battery of functional mobility and balance tests, including a limited battery of FMS tests (deep squat, hurdle step, and inline lunge). Testing consisted of three conditions: control, EOD suit, and weighted vest. Experimental conditions (EOD and vest) were randomized to avoid fatigue and the repeated bout effect. A repeated measures analysis of variance (ANOVA) was performed, accompanied by Tukey's Honestly Significant Differences (HSD) post-hoc test, to assess differences in FMS scores and RPE ratings across the three conditions.
